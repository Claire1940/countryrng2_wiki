<Section icon={Target} title="Rare Countries, Flags, and Rarity Signals">

The main draw in Country RNG 2 is chasing rare countries and showing them off as flags. Normal results help you get started, but the real value comes from the pulls that are hard to find and worth recording.

<Callout type="warning" title="Rarity Rule">

| Result Type | What It Means | Best Response |

| Rare country | High-value chase result | Stop, record, and compare |

The safest way to think about rarity is simple: if a result feels important enough to show someone, treat it as a milestone and keep a clean record. That habit pays off later when you compare your progression route.

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Luck and Upgrade Tree Priority">

Luck and the upgrade tree do different jobs. Luck improves your odds of reaching stronger results, while the upgrade tree supports long-term growth and better flag progression. The best early route is to strengthen the main loop before chasing convenience.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Build the core loop">
    Roll repeatedly until the game’s pace feels natural. Learn how often results change and how quickly your collection grows.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Invest in the upgrade tree">
    Push the upgrade tree early because it supports future progress and better flags. Treat it as your main growth path.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Add luck after the base loop feels stable">
    Use luck when you want better odds for rare-country hunting. It matters most once you already understand the roll cycle.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Use speed and income as support systems">
    Roll speed, cash, gold, and potion output are useful when they remove a bottleneck. Do not stack them blindly.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Do Not Overbuild">
A fast setup is not always a smart setup. If you do not know whether cash, gold, or roll speed is your real bottleneck, spend on the wrong layer and slow yourself down.
</Callout>

| Progression Layer | Best Use | Early Priority | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Upgrade Tree | Core account growth | **High** | Main route for better flags |
| Luck | Rare-country hunting | **High** | Most useful when chasing strong pulls |
| Roll Speed | More attempts per session | Medium | Helps volume, not single-roll odds |
| Cash | Faster spending on upgrades | Medium | Great when costs start blocking progress |
| Gold | Another growth resource | Medium | Best when gold is the next bottleneck |
| Potion Roll | Separate boost layer | Low to Medium | Strong later, not usually first purchase |

The game’s pass lineup matches this structure: 2X Luck, Faster Rolls, 2X Cash, 2X Gold, and 3X Potion Roll all speed up a specific layer instead of replacing the whole progression path.

<Callout type="success">
When in doubt, prioritize the layer that lets you roll more often without breaking your upgrade economy.
</Callout>
