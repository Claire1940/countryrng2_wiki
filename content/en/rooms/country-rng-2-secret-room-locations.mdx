<Section icon={Compass} title="How to Search Hidden Rooms Efficiently">

Use a fixed route instead of wandering. That gives you cleaner results, faster repeat checks, and better notes when you compare servers later. A hidden room is much easier to document when you inspect the same path in the same order every time.

**Route Checklist Table**

| Step | Action | Result You Want |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Enter the area and pause at the first junction | Build a mental map before moving fast |
| 2 | Sweep the outer walls clockwise | Catch side openings and tucked entrances |
| 3 | Inspect every dead end twice | Avoid missing thin openings or low-detail corners |
| 4 | Check vertical spaces | Find stairs, ledges, and upper hidden spots |
| 5 | Re-run the same path on another server | Confirm whether the room is consistent |

  <Step num="1" title="Start with a controlled entry">

  <Step num="2" title="Scan the outer boundary">

  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Double-check every dead end">

**Room Signal Table**

| Signal | Strong or Weak | What It Suggests |
|---|---|---|

| One-time visual clip | Weak | Could be a glitch or camera angle issue |
| No access on a second server | Weak | Needs more testing before logging |
